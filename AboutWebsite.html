Our Website

Mission Statement:
The main goal of our website is to bring about higher awareness of the issues that are currently transpiring within the New York Harbor ecosystem and surrounding areas, especially where the population of oysters is concerned. This website also hopes to help spread the message that the program, Billion Oyster Project has already begun to communicate. The mission of the Billion Oyster Project, located on Governor's Island, is to take the first step towards repairing the damage inflicted on the oyster population within the New York Harbor by raising a population of 1 billion oysters within the harbor. Additionally, his website also seeks to provide a comprehensible format in which viewers can visualize the data associated with the Billion Oyster Project. It aims to supply and collect virtual forms that will gather information concerning the oysters grown on Governor's Island in New York City by the Billion Oyster Project.

Background of Oysters in New York Harbor:
Pollution has become a significant issue within the New York Harbor area due to many factors such as waste and chemical disposal in the Hudson River. One of these types of chemicals, polychlorinated biphenyls, or otherwise known as PCBs, were one kind of the infamous chemicals that were released into the Hudson River. Fortunately, EPA prohibited the development of PCBs in 1977, and in 2002, EPA declared a ROD, or Record of Decision that aimed to remove over 2 million cubic yards of sediment residing at the bottom of the river that was infilitrated with these chemicals. Despite what has already been done to help improve the river ecosystem, more actions are still urgently needed to bring about the restoration of the New York Harbor.

Over the years as pollution levels increased within the harbor, oyster population levels have drastically declined and as a result, the rest of the ecosystem has also deteriorated. The oyster population plays a critical role in the health and well-being of the rest of New York Harbor environment. Without a rehabilitation in the levels of oysters, the rest of the harbor habitat will suffer the effects. Oysters have been known to bring many benefits to water ecosystems such as filtering up to 50 gallons of water a day each, which can assist in greatly improving the quality of water, and removing plankton, bacteria, and other organic substances, ensuring a better regulation of the ecosystem's natural cycle. By bringing back the oyster population to the New York Harbor area, the ecosystem will profit appreciably and will, hopefully, one day thrive again.
